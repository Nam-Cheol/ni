# Lessons

The research-protocol case matters because the original request is not another
software surface with missing product details. It asks a team to plan work that
could affect residents, safety, privacy, consent, and field operations. That
makes premature handoff riskier than simply building the wrong screen.

## Why This Case Matters

- It shows that ni is not software-only.
- It tests intent readiness before human team handoff and before research
  fieldwork planning.
- It keeps research planning at the contract boundary instead of turning ni
  into an execution or fieldwork system.
- It demonstrates that a vague request can sound practical while still missing
  evidence needed for safe research planning.

## What ni Surfaced

- Research question ambiguity: the request did not specify what would be
  learned, compared, or accepted as evidence.
- Participant and observation scope: eligible areas, groups, and observation
  windows were not accepted.
- Privacy, consent, and data boundaries: participant contact, personal data,
  translation, accessibility, and storage rules needed explicit treatment.
- Field safety and weather stop rules: extreme heat creates conditions where
  fieldwork must stop or change.
- Reviewer and acceptance evidence: the team needed a named review owner and
  clear pass/fail evidence before handoff.

## Why Synthetic Answers Are Acceptable Here

- They are clearly labeled as benchmark fixture data.
- They are used only to test the ni readiness transition from `BLOCKED` to
  `READY`.
- They are not real approval, fieldwork authorization, ethics review, or
  research quality evidence.
- They let the benchmark verify status, lock, and bounded prompt behavior
  without pretending that a real study is ready.

## Why This Strengthens ni's Positioning

- ni is useful before a human team takes ownership, not only before an AI agent
  starts coding.
- ni is useful for non-software planning where unclear intent can create safety
  and governance risk.
- ni remains non-execution-oriented: it gates intent, locks accepted planning
  artifacts, and compiles bounded handoff seed material.
- ni can support research fieldwork planning by forcing unresolved scope,
  safety, privacy, and reviewer questions into the open before handoff.

## Practical Takeaways

- Keep the original `BLOCKED` proof beside the resolved fixture proof.
- Preserve `not_measured` boundaries even after a workspace reaches `READY`.
- Treat research approval and fieldwork authorization as external governance
  steps, not as side effects of ni readiness.
- Do not execute the compiled prompt as part of this benchmark; execution would
  measure a downstream system, not the Intent Lock Protocol.
