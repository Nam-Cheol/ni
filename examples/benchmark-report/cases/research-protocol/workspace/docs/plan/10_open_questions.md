# Open questions

## OQ-001: What research question, supported decision, and final study artifact should the neighborhood cooling study produce?

Blocker: false

Status: resolved

Resolution: Synthetic benchmark fixture answer. The research question asks
which public outdoor blocks in the fictional Riverside East and Oak Market
corridors appear to need further shade or cooling intervention review based on
observed shade deficit, public-space heat exposure, and non-identifying
community feedback. The supported decision is a short list of candidate blocks
for later design review, owned by the City Resilience Program planning owner.
The final artifact is a planning memo with the question, candidate-block
shortlist, evidence table, privacy and safety boundaries, limitations, and a
pre-fieldwork readiness checklist. The study must not decide final
intervention placement, construction scope, procurement, budget approval,
public-health claims, clinical heat-risk conclusions, or production deployment.

## OQ-002: Which participant or observation scope, inclusion and exclusion criteria, locations, and observation windows are accepted?

Blocker: false

Status: resolved

Resolution: Synthetic benchmark fixture answer. Observation units are public
street segments, public plazas, transit-adjacent waiting areas, and publicly
accessible pedestrian corridors. Optional participant interaction is limited to
adult community members who voluntarily give non-identifying feedback. The
scope includes outdoor public locations in Riverside East or Oak Market with
visible pedestrian use, low shade coverage, or prior public planning interest.
It excludes private property, schools, care facilities, indoor spaces,
locations requiring special access, unsafe observation locations, and any
interaction involving minors. Observation happens weekdays between 09:00 and
16:00 during the summer study period only when field safety conditions permit.
Select up to 12 public segments using shade deficit, pedestrian activity, and
planning relevance, and record why each segment was included.

## OQ-003: What consent, privacy, data handling, translation, accessibility, and sensitive-data boundaries apply?

Blocker: false

Status: resolved

Resolution: Synthetic benchmark fixture answer. Allowed data is public-space
observation notes, segment-level shade observations, public infrastructure
notes, non-identifying voluntary comments, public map references, and
aggregated segment-level evidence. Prohibited data includes names, contact
information, photos of identifiable people, license plates, medical or health
data, household-level data, precise resident addresses, credentials, tokens,
private customer data, and raw audio/video. Voluntary comments use a short
plain-language consent script that says comments are optional,
non-identifying, and used only for planning evidence; comments from minors are
not collected. Notes stay aggregated at segment level, raw notes are not
published, raw field notes are reduced after up to 30 days, and the final memo
keeps only aggregated non-identifying evidence. English and Spanish materials,
plain language, readable font size, accessible routes, and non-digital
participation are required.

## OQ-004: What field-team safety rules, heat or weather stop rules, vulnerable-group safeguards, and escalation path are required?

Blocker: false

Status: resolved

Resolution: Synthetic benchmark fixture answer. Field teams work in pairs,
carry water, have charged phones, check weather before departure, and stay in
public areas only. Cancel or pause fieldwork if heat index exceeds 38 C, an
official heat emergency is active, lightning occurs within 10 miles, air
quality is unhealthy, or the field lead judges conditions unsafe. Maximum
continuous outdoor observation is 30 minutes followed by at least 10 minutes of
shaded or indoor rest; maximum total outdoor observation time is 3 hours per
field day. Do not target minors, elderly residents, unhoused residents, or
medically vulnerable people for comments, and do not ask health-status
questions. Field team contacts the field lead first; for urgent danger, call
local emergency services. Extreme heat, unsafe air quality, lightning, civil
safety concerns, lack of two-person team, lack of water/rest access, or unsafe
site conditions cancel fieldwork.

## OQ-005: Who reviews the protocol, what acceptance evidence is required, and what must be ready before fieldwork can be considered?

Blocker: false

Status: resolved

Resolution: Synthetic benchmark fixture answer. The City Resilience Program
planning owner plus a privacy/safety reviewer jointly review the protocol for
the planning owner, field lead, privacy/safety reviewer, and community
engagement lead. The minimum protocol artifact is a protocol memo covering the
research question, location selection rule, observation scope, consent script,
privacy boundaries, safety stop rules, non-goals, risk mitigations, and
acceptance checklist. Acceptance evidence requires all OQ fields answered, high
risks mitigated, non-goals explicit, consent/privacy/safety sections reviewed,
fieldwork stop rules documented, and `ni status` returning `READY` or
`READY_WITH_DEFERRALS` in the isolated benchmark workspace. Actual fieldwork,
participant recruitment, data collection, statistical analysis, final
intervention placement, budget approval, dashboard/software implementation,
and downstream agent execution are explicitly not required.
