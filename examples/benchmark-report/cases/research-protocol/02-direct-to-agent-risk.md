# Direct-to-agent risk

If the vague request were handed directly to a downstream agent or team, they
would likely need to invent important research intent before beginning.

Likely invented assumptions:

- Research question assumed: the downstream actor may decide what "cooling
  study" means without an accepted research question or supported decision.
- Neighborhoods assumed: locations, observation windows, and inclusion or
  exclusion criteria may be guessed.
- Participants assumed: the actor may infer who may be contacted or observed,
  including vulnerable groups, without accepted safeguards.
- Data sources assumed: environmental observations, interviews, surveys,
  sensors, maps, demographic data, or prior reports may be selected without
  accepted authority.
- Consent/privacy ignored: the actor may treat participant contact or personal
  data as permissible without consent, privacy, retention, or de-identification
  rules.
- Field safety ignored: heat/weather stop rules, field-team safety ownership,
  and escalation paths may be missing.
- Intervention criteria invented: shade or cooling placement criteria may be
  invented before the study method and evidence threshold are accepted.
- Acceptance evidence undefined: no reviewer, pass/fail criteria, ethics
  checkpoint, or pre-fieldwork readiness gate is named.

Direct execution would be unsafe because it could turn unresolved research
questions into implicit fieldwork, participant contact, or intervention
recommendations.
